sources & methodology

The investigation “Confiscated goods in Europe” started with the aim of gathering, analyzing and visualizing available data on confiscated goods in EU member states. A look at the main institutional sources allowed us to claim that a database including data and stats on the number and value of confiscated goods does not yet exist.
The collection of data took place in each country using the following sources:

  • ITALY - Average number of confiscated goods through anti-money laundering prevention measures - real estate and companies - between 2011/2012 and 2014/2015. Average value of confiscated goods through anti-money laundering prevention measures - real estate and companies between 2011/2012 and 2014/2015. Source: Data gathered by the Agency of Seized and Confiscated Goods and processed by the Ministry of Interior (referring to the period between August 1 and July 31). http://www.interno.gov.it/sites/default/files/ppv_ferragosto_2015_stampa_0.pdf
  • ENGLAND AND WALES - Number and value (in pound sterling) of confiscated goods in 2014/2015 (between 1 April and 31 March). Source: Ministry of Justice data provided by the HM Courts and Tribunal Services following a Freedom of Information request.
  • GERMANY - Value of seized and confiscated goods in 2014. Source: Bundeskriminalamt.
  • SPAIN - Number of confiscated goods in 2014, including cash. Source: Plan Nacional Sobre Drogas. Number of goods and value of confiscated cash in the Autonomous Communities of Spain in 2014. Source: Plan Nacional Sobre Drogas. Estimate of value of confiscated goods in 2013, including cash. Source: Processing by Transcrime-Universidad Rey Juan Carlos based on data by PNSD
  • FRANCE - Number of seized and confiscated goods, including cash (average value for years 2011-2013). Source: Report 2013 Agrasc (Agence de gestion et de recouvrement des avoirs saisis et confisqués) - Agency for the Recovery and the Management of seized and confiscated goods. Number of goods. Number of seized and confiscated goods and value of cash seized and confiscated by region - years 2011-2012. Source: Agrasc Report 2012

Sources used in the making of the infographics illustrating legislations in the 28 different EU Member States. Disposal of confiscated assets in the Eu Member States - Law and Practices, written by the Center for the Study of Democracy. We have devised ten questions on existing laws regulating the confiscation and re-use of criminal assets. Answers are identified by the following colours: Yes (Light blue), No (Red), Not Available N.A. (White)

About

Confiscated Goods is a project by Dataninja.it, partially funded by JournalismFund.eu and developed by an international consortium of journalists (Gianluca De Martino, Andrea Nelson Mauro, Alessio Cimarelli, Daniele Grasso, Alexandre Lechenet, Dirk Liedtke, Frederique Mallard). The project aims to make more trasparency on seizing and management of criminal assets in EU.

Confiscated Goods is a part of ConfiscatiBene, a project promoted by Ondata.it, an Italian indipendent NGO that works for spreading ahead transparency and OpenData due to disseminating digital culture and data skills with off-line and on-line activities. Ondata brings skills both from journalism and coding to scale up its iniziatives and mission.
